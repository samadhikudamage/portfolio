3D Illustration

Isometric House Design

Project Overview

This project represents a comprehensive exploration of isometric design principles applied to architectural visualization. The goal was to create a series of detailed 3D illustrations that showcase modern house designs using clean geometric forms and contemporary color palettes.

The design process involved extensive research into modern architectural trends, careful attention to lighting and shadow relationships, and the development of a cohesive visual language that could be applied across multiple house designs. Each illustration was crafted to maintain perfect geometric accuracy while remaining visually appealing and easy to understand.

Design Process

The project began with sketching basic isometric grids and exploring different house configurations. I focused on creating designs that would work well both as standalone pieces and as part of a larger collection. The color palette was carefully selected to provide good contrast while maintaining a modern, sophisticated feel.

Key Features

  • Perfect isometric perspective throughout all elements
  • Consistent lighting and shadow system
  • Modern architectural details and proportions
  • Scalable vector-based artwork
  • Cohesive color scheme across the series

Technical Challenges

One of the main challenges was maintaining the perfect isometric angles while creating visually interesting compositions. I developed a custom grid system that ensured all elements remained geometrically accurate while allowing for creative freedom in the overall design.
